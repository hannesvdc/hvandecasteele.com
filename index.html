Hello! I am a researcher with strong interests in computational science and machine learning for science. I currently work in quantitative finance, applying statistical modeling and high-performance computing to understanding markets. In addition to research and engineering, I regularly give talks and enjoy discussing computational science, scientific machine learning, and their practical impact. I also lead Open Numerics, an independent computational science initiative through which I advise organizations on advanced numerical modeling, scientific machine learning, and large-scale computation.

My current research focuses on developing machine learning methods for scientific computing, with a particular emphasis on physics-informed learning, time-series prediction, and computational chemistry. More broadly, I am interested in combining modern machine learning with numerical analysis, stochastic modeling, and multiscale methods to tackle problems that are otherwise computationally intractable, including challenges arising in drug discovery. As part of my research, I contribute to open source projects regularly.

Prior to transitioning to industry, I was a postdoctoral research fellow in the Department of Applied Mathematics and Statistics at Johns Hopkins University (JHU), with a secondary appointment in the Department of Chemical and Biomolecular Engineering. I worked under the supervision of dr. Ioannis Kevrekidis.

I earned my PhD from KU Leuven (Belgium) in 2023. My professional background also includes experience as a software engineer at Facebook, where I worked on physics engines for virtual reality applications, as well as independent consulting work for energy companies.
